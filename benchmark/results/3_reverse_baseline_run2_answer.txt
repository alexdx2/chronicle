## Producers

**`BattleResultProducer`** — `arena-api`
- File: `arena-api/src/arena/battle-result.producer.ts`
- Class: `BattleResultProducer` (injectable NestJS service using `kafkajs`)
- Publishing is triggered inside `ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry()` (`arena-api/src/arena/arena.service.ts`) — called every time a battle attack is processed
- Entry point: `POST /arena/attack` (handled by `ArenaController`)

---

## Consumers

**`BattleResultConsumer`** — `spectators-api`
- File: `spectators-api/src/spectators/battle-result.consumer.ts`
- Class: `BattleResultConsumer`, uses `@EventPattern('battle-results')` from `@nestjs/microservices`
- Handler method: `handleBattleResult(event)` → immediately delegates to `SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`

---

## Downstream Flow

After `spectators-api` consumes a message from `battle-results`:

1. **`SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`** appends the event to an in-memory battles array.

2. That stored data then feeds three downstream operations:
   - **`GET /stats/leaderboard`** — aggregates wins for Tom (`TOM_HITS`) and Jerry (`JERRY_DODGES`, `TRAP_TRIGGERED`)
   - **`GET /stats/recent`** — returns the last 10 battle events
   - **`CleanupTask` (cron job)** — runs on two schedules:
     - Hourly: cleanup routine
     - Daily at midnight: calls `getLeaderboard()` to generate a daily report

The message payload itself (loosely typed as `any`) carries the structure created by `arena-api`'s Prisma `BattleEvent` record: `{ tomId, jerryId, weapon, trap, result, damage, id, createdAt }`.
