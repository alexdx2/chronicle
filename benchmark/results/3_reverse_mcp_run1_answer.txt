Here's the complete breakdown of the `battle-results` Kafka topic:

---

## Producers

**`BattleResultProducer`** (`arena-api/src/arena/battle-result.producer.ts`)
- Hardcodes the topic name `'battle-results'` (line 3)
- Exposes a single `publish(event: any)` method
- Is injected into `ArenaService` via constructor and called at the end of `tomAttacksJerry()`

---

## Consumers

**`BattleResultConsumer`** (`spectators-api/src/spectators/battle-result.consumer.ts`)
- Uses `@EventPattern('battle-results')` (NestJS microservices decorator) to subscribe
- Handler method: `handleBattleResult(event: any)`
- Delegates immediately to `SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`

---

## Downstream Flow

1. **Trigger**: `ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry(weaponType)` is called (via an HTTP endpoint in `arena-api`)
2. **Battle logic runs**: Checks Jerry's traps, compares speeds, computes `result` (`TRAP_TRIGGERED` | `JERRY_DODGES` | `TOM_HITS`) and `damage`
3. **Persisted to DB**: A `BattleEvent` record is written to Prisma/SQLite in `arena-api` *before* publishing
4. **Published to Kafka**: `BattleResultProducer.publish(event)` sends the persisted `BattleEvent` to `battle-results`
5. **Consumed by spectators-api**: `BattleResultConsumer.handleBattleResult()` receives the event and calls `SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`, which appends it to an in-memory `battles[]` array
6. **Available for read queries**: `SpectatorService` exposes:
   - `getLeaderboard()` — counts Tom wins vs. Jerry wins from the in-memory array
   - `getRecentBattles()` — returns last 10 events from the array

> **Note:** `spectators-api`'s state is in-memory only — it does not persist to a database. It also has `TOM_API_URL` and `JERRY_API_URL` env vars configured in `SpectatorService`, but those are used for name lookups (`getTomName()`, `getJerryName()`), not for the Kafka event flow.

---

## Services Involved

| Service | Role |
|---|---|
| **`arena-api`** | **Producer** — owns battle logic, persists `BattleEvent` to its own DB, then publishes to `battle-results` |
| **`spectators-api`** | **Consumer** — receives battle events, maintains in-memory leaderboard and recent-battle history |
| **`tom-api`** | Indirectly involved — `arena-api` calls `TomClient.getStatus()` *before* the battle to fetch Tom's speed/ID, but does not interact with Kafka |
| **`jerry-api`** | Indirectly involved — `arena-api` calls `JerryClient.getStatus()` and `getTraps()` *before* the battle, but does not interact with Kafka |
