Here's the complete picture of the `battle-results` Kafka topic, verified directly from source:

---

## Producers

- **`BattleResultProducer`** — `arena-api/src/arena/battle-result.producer.ts`
  - Publishes to the `battle-results` topic via `publish(event)`.
  - Triggered internally by `ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry()` after a battle outcome is determined and persisted to the database. The event payload is a `BattleEvent` Prisma record containing `tomId`, `jerryId`, `weapon`, `trap`, `result` (`TOM_HITS` | `JERRY_DODGES` | `TRAP_TRIGGERED`), and `damage`.

---

## Consumers

- **`BattleResultConsumer`** — `spectators-api/src/spectators/battle-result.consumer.ts`
  - Listens on `@EventPattern('battle-results')`.
  - On receipt, calls `SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`, which appends the event to an in-memory `battles[]` array.

---

## Downstream Flow

```
[HTTP] POST /arena/attack
       │
       ▼
ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry()
  ├─ fetches Tom & Jerry status (HTTP → tom-api, jerry-api)
  ├─ fetches Jerry's traps (HTTP → jerry-api)
  ├─ computes result: TOM_HITS | JERRY_DODGES | TRAP_TRIGGERED
  ├─ persists BattleEvent → Prisma (arena-api DB)
  └─ BattleResultProducer.publish(event)
                │
                ▼
        Kafka: battle-results
                │
                ▼
        BattleResultConsumer.handleBattleResult(event)
                │
                ▼
        SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)
          └─ appends to in-memory battles[]
                │
                ▼
        Powers spectators-api endpoints:
          GET /spectators/leaderboard  → win counts by TOM_HITS / JERRY_DODGES / TRAP_TRIGGERED
          GET /spectators/recent       → last 10 battle events
```

---

## Services Involved

| Service | Role |
|---|---|
| **`arena-api`** | **Producer** — orchestrates battles, persists results, publishes to Kafka |
| **`spectators-api`** | **Consumer** — receives battle events, maintains in-memory leaderboard and recent-battles state |
| **`tom-api`** | Indirect — queried by `arena-api` during battle resolution for Tom's status |
| **`jerry-api`** | Indirect — queried by `arena-api` during battle resolution for Jerry's status and active traps |

> **Note:** `spectators-api`'s leaderboard is in-memory only (`battles[]` array) — it is not persisted to a database. This means state is lost on service restart, and it relies entirely on receiving Kafka events to stay current.
