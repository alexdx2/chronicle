Here is the complete, verified breakdown of everything connected to the `battle-results` Kafka topic:

---

## Producers

**`BattleResultProducer`** вЂ” `arena-api/src/arena/battle-result.producer.ts`

- Publishes to the topic via `publish(event)` after every attack resolution in `ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry()`.
- The event payload is a persisted `BattleEvent` Prisma record containing: `tomId`, `jerryId`, `weapon`, `trap`, `result` (`TOM_HITS` | `JERRY_DODGES` | `TRAP_TRIGGERED`), `damage`, and `createdAt`.
- Belongs to: **`arena-api`** (via `ArenaModule`)

---

## Consumers

**`BattleResultConsumer`** вЂ” `spectators-api/src/spectators/battle-result.consumer.ts`

- Decorated with `@EventPattern('battle-results')` вЂ” subscribes to every message on the topic.
- Handler: `handleBattleResult(event)` в†’ delegates immediately to `SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)`, which appends the event to an in-memory `battles[]` array.
- Belongs to: **`spectators-api`** (via `SpectatorsModule`)

---

## Downstream Flow

```
POST /arena/attack (arena-api)
  в””в”Ђв–є ArenaService.tomAttacksJerry()
        в”њв”Ђ Fetches Tom status (tom-api GET /tom/status)
        в”њв”Ђ Fetches Jerry status + traps (jerry-api GET /jerry/status, GET /jerry/traps)
        в”њв”Ђ Resolves outcome (TOM_HITS / JERRY_DODGES / TRAP_TRIGGERED)
        в”њв”Ђ Persists BattleEvent to Prisma (arena-api DB)
        в””в”Ђв–є BattleResultProducer.publish(event)
              в””в”Ђв–є Kafka topic: battle-results
                    в””в”Ђв–є BattleResultConsumer.handleBattleResult(event)  [spectators-api]
                          в””в”Ђв–є SpectatorService.recordBattle(event)
                                в””в”Ђ battles[] (in-memory store)
                                      в”њв”Ђв–є GET /stats/leaderboard  в†’  win counts for Tom & Jerry
                                      в””в”Ђв–є GET /stats/recent       в†’  last 10 battle events
```

After a message is published:
1. **`spectators-api`** receives it and stores it in memory.
2. The aggregated data immediately becomes available via two HTTP endpoints:
   - `GET /stats/leaderboard` вЂ” returns `{ tom: N, jerry: N, total: N }` (win counts computed by filtering `result` values)
   - `GET /stats/recent` вЂ” returns the last 10 battle events

Note: `arena-api` also has its own independent score/history endpoints (`GET /arena/score`, `GET /arena/history`) that read directly from Prisma вЂ” those do **not** go through the Kafka topic.

## Services Involved

| Service | Role |
|---|---|
| **`arena-api`** | **Producer** вЂ” triggers publish after resolving each attack; also owns the Prisma-backed `BattleEvent` table |
| **`spectators-api`** | **Consumer** вЂ” ingests events, maintains in-memory leaderboard, exposes `/stats/*` endpoints |
| **`tom-api`** | Upstream data source вЂ” queried by `arena-api` during attack resolution (not directly on the Kafka path) |
| **`jerry-api`** | Upstream data source вЂ” queried by `arena-api` during attack resolution (not directly on the Kafka path) |
